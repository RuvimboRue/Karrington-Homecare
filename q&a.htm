Karrington Private Homecare is defined by the Care Quality Commission (CQC) as an Introductory Agency, in line with the Health and Social Act 2008. This means even though we recruit carers, verify their care qualifications and skills, check their immigration status and right to work, check with DBS if they have a criminal history, check their work references, we do not manage the care that is given to the client. We strongly believe that care should be managed by those who know the client best, and that is the family. We also encourage that care should be overseen by people on the ground, in this case it would be the family and the carer. Having worked as carers ourselves, we believe care should not be managed by a remote manager with 25 other clients to manage, but the people who are physically spending time with the person needing support, and it's always the family and the carer. We believe all our carers are equipped with knowledge, skill and initiative to provide the best care for the client together working with the family. This is why we encourage good communication between family and carer.

If you are not happy with your carer, please get in touch with us, we will make other arrangements.
