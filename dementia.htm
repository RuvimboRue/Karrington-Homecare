Dementia/Alzheimers Care

Dementia and Alzheimers disease are not the same. Symptoms of the two conditions can overlap, that is why its very important to get a diagnosis for a loved one so they can get the best treatment.

Dementia is a syndrome, not a disease. Dementia affects mental cognitive tasks such as memory and reasoning. It can happen for a variety of reasons, the most common being Alzheimers disease.

Loved ones affected by Advanced Dementia show aggression, can be verbally abusive, have difficulty remembering family members or places and unable to care for themselves.

Advanced Alzheimers disease sufferers will have difficulty speaking, swallowing and walking. Depression, forgetfulness and confusion can affect both conditions.

Karrington Private Homecare can connect families to Dementia/Alzheimers trained Live in carers who are kind, sympathetic, understanding, patient and who are fully aware that the aggression and verbal abuse is a result of Dementia.
